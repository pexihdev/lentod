# Correction Policy

_Last updated: July 11, 2026_

At **Lentod**, we are committed to providing accurate, reliable, and useful information to our readers.

Despite our best efforts, mistakes may occasionally occur. This Correction Policy explains how we handle errors, updates, and improvements to our published content.

## Our Commitment to Accuracy

We aim to publish content that is:

- Accurate
- Well-researched
- Clear
- Relevant
- Helpful for our readers

We continuously review and improve our content as technology and information evolve.

## Types of Corrections

Corrections may include:

### Factual Errors

Changes made when published information contains incorrect facts, statistics, names, dates, technical details, or other inaccurate information.

### Technical Corrections

Updates made when:

- Software behavior changes.
- Code examples require updates.
- APIs or frameworks change.
- Documentation becomes outdated.
- Security recommendations need improvement.

### Editorial Corrections

Changes made to improve:

- Grammar
- Spelling
- Formatting
- Clarity
- Readability

### Link Corrections

We may update or remove:

- Broken links
- Incorrect references
- Outdated resources

## How We Handle Reported Errors

When a reader reports a possible error, we will:

1. Review the reported information.
2. Verify the claim using reliable sources.
3. Determine whether a correction is required.
4. Update the content when appropriate.

Not every suggestion will result in a correction if the original information is accurate.

## How to Report a Correction

If you find an error in any Lentod article, please provide:

- Article URL
- Description of the issue
- The incorrect information
- Suggested correction
- Supporting sources, if available

Detailed reports help us review issues more effectively.

Please contact us through our Contact page.

## Correction Transparency

When a significant correction affects the meaning or accuracy of an article, we may:

- Update the content.
- Add clarification.
- Include a correction note.
- Update the article date when appropriate.

Minor corrections such as spelling or formatting improvements may not require a public notice.

## Article Updates

Technology content can become outdated quickly.

We may update older articles to:

- Reflect newer software versions.
- Replace outdated recommendations.
- Improve technical explanations.
- Add additional examples.
- Remove obsolete information.

## Security Corrections

Security-related errors are treated with priority.

If incorrect security information is discovered, we will review and update the content as quickly as reasonably possible.

## AI-Assisted Content Corrections

Some Lentod content may use AI-assisted tools during research, drafting, or editing.

All published content remains subject to editorial review.

If an AI-assisted article contains incorrect information, we will correct it according to the same standards applied to all content.

## Reader Contributions

We appreciate readers who help improve Lentod by reporting errors and suggesting improvements.

Constructive feedback helps maintain the quality of our platform.

## Our Responsibility

We take responsibility for correcting mistakes when they are identified.

However, due to the rapidly changing nature of technology, we cannot guarantee that all information will remain permanently current.

## Changes to This Policy

We may update this Correction Policy periodically.

Any changes will be published on this page with an updated revision date.

## Contact

If you have identified an error or have a correction request, please contact Lentod through our Contact page.

Thank you for helping us improve the accuracy and quality of Lentod.
